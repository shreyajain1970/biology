  • vertebrates store glycogen mainly in liver and muscle cells.
  • energy stored by carbohydrates can sustain a human for at most one day unless they are replenish by eating
  • low carbohydrate diets can result in weakness and fatigue

Primary structure :

  • the precise primary structure of a protein is determined not by the random linking of amino acids, but by inherited genetic information.
Secondary Structure:
  • Many proteins have segments of their polypeptide chains repeatedly cioled or folded in patterns that contribute to the protein's overall shape. These coils and folds, collectively referred to as secondary structure.

  • these hydrogen bonds are weak, but because they are repeated many times over a relatively long region of the polypeptide chain, they can support a particular shape for that part of the protein.
